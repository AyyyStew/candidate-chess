# Candidate Chess is live

After a few weeks of hacking on this in my spare time, Candidate Chess is now live at [candidatechess.com](https://candidatechess.com).

## What it is

The idea is simple: you're shown a chess position and asked to find the engine's top 5 moves, Family Feud style. No eval bar. No hints. Just you and the board.

The goal is to force deliberate candidate move thinking before reaching for the engine — something I noticed I was completely skipping in my own games.

## How it works

Select positions are filtered through a custom pipeline to find instructive middlegame positions. The full pipeline is [open source on GitHub](https://github.com/AyyyStew/candidate-chess/tree/master/position_generation).
This pipeline includes precomputed Stockfish 18 results to use as the engine's top moves.

For moves outside of the precomputed moves the app itself runs Stockfish in the browser via WASM to verify your guesses in real time.

Built by an 800 elo player, so take everything with a grain of salt. Feedback welcome on [GitHub](https://github.com/AyyyStew/candidate-chess).
